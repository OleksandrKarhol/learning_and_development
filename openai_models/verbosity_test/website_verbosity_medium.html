About

A studio built around craft and sourcing.

Atelier Grove is a small team of makers and designers. We work in hardwoods that are responsibly harvested, dimensioned carefully, and finished to be lived with.

Story

We began with a simple preference: fewer objects, better made. Our forms are modern, but our methods come from the bench—careful layout, sharp tools, and joints that hold without drama.

Our sourcing is equally deliberate. We choose mills and suppliers that prioritize sustainable forestry, then select boards with stability and character—matching grain, tone, and movement to the intended piece.

Process

Three steps, executed with consistency.

Discuss a project
  1. 01 Design

    Proportion and clarity

    We start with measured drawings and a concise spec—dimensions, wood, finish, and functional requirements.

  2. 02 Build

    Joinery and fit

    Boards are milled in stages for stability; joints are cut for clean shoulders and long-term strength.

  3. 03 Finish

    A durable, calm surface

    We use low-VOC oils and hardwax where appropriate—selected for repairability and a natural, tactile sheen.

The atelier

A bright workshop, sharp hand tools, and dedicated milling and finishing zones—kept tidy so the work stays precise.

Sustainability, as practice

Less promise, more process.

We prioritize domestic hardwoods from responsibly managed forests, plan cuts to reduce waste, and choose finishes that extend service life. When possible, we design parts to be repaired—so the piece can stay with you, not cycle out.

Packaging is minimal and recyclable; offcuts are reused for jigs, test panels, and small objects.
