ABOUT

A small atelier with a disciplined hand.

Atelier Grove began as a two-person workshop focused on one principle: well-considered furniture should feel inevitable—nothing added, nothing missing. We design around human movement, architectural sightlines, and the honest behavior of solid wood.

Our hardwoods come from responsibly managed forests and small mills where boards are air-dried with patience. Each piece is built to be serviced—hardware accessible, finishes renewable, and joinery designed for decades of seasonal change.

Process

From sketch to final rub-out

  1. 01 1–2 weeks

    Design

    Proportions, edge profiles, and grain direction finalized with clear drawings and samples.

  2. 02 3–6 weeks

    Build

    Milled, rested, and joined. Allowances for movement are built in—not hidden later.

  3. 03 1–2 weeks

    Finish

    Low-VOC oil and wax, hand-rubbed between coats for a calm sheen and tactile clarity.

A calm place to make careful things.

We prototype details at full scale—drawer reveals, pulls, edge radii—until the piece reads as one continuous decision.

SUSTAINABILITY

We prioritize certified lumber, local milling when possible, and packaging designed for reuse. Offcuts are sorted for future jigs, small goods, or donation.

Care, not consumption.

We provide finish care cards and offer refresh services to extend the life of each piece.
