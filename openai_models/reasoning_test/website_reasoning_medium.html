About

A small atelier, a clear standard.

Story

Atelier Grove began with a simple premise: furniture should be structurally honest and visually quiet. We draw from cabinetmaking traditions, then refine the lines for contemporary rooms—proportions first, ornament last.

Our hardwoods come from FSC-certified sources and regional mills where possible. We select boards for stability and figure, then build in batches to maintain consistency—grain-matched panels, balanced color, and a finish schedule tuned to the wood.

Process

Three steps, repeated carefully.

  1. 01

    Design

    Measured drawings, material selection, and a final spec you can keep.

  2. 02

    Build

    Joinery-led construction, dry fits, and careful grain alignment.

  3. 03

    Finish

    Low-VOC oils and waxes; burnished edges; final inspection in daylight.

Sustainability

Responsible materials, measured waste.

We prioritize certified forestry, regional sourcing where feasible, and finishes with low VOC content. Offcuts are repurposed for jigs, wedges, and small goods; sawdust is collected for compost partners. Longevity is the core strategy: pieces built to be repaired, refinished, and kept.
