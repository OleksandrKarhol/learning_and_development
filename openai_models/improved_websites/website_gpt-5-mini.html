Our story

Atelier Grove began as a small studio focused on making one enduring piece at a time. We believe furniture should serve daily life while gaining character, not fading with trend cycles. Each project is approached with intentional restraint — forms that are calm, joinery that endures, and finishes that reveal the wood’s natural depth.

We source hardwoods from responsibly managed forests and seek reclaimed timbers where possible. Relationships with mills, arborists, and local suppliers help us minimize waste and prioritize timber with a meaningful provenance.

Sustainability

We balance craft and stewardship: selective sourcing, long-lasting finishes, and repair services to extend a piece's life. Our workshop minimizes offcuts and turns salvage into smaller objects or studio supplies.

Our process

  1. Design — considered beginning

    We begin with measurements and sketches, balancing scale, ergonomics, and the interplay of solid surfaces and negative space.

  2. Build — joinery & craft

    Hand and machine work converge. Joinery is selected for longevity—mortise and tenon, dovetail, and through-tenon details are common.

  3. Finish — enduring patina

    We use oils and waxes that protect and allow the wood to age gracefully, promoting a tactile, low-sheen surface.
